Why Choose A Slate Roof?

Slate Roof Benefits

  • Superior Durability: Slate roofs are renowned for their exceptional durability, making them ideal for the harsh weather conditions of New England. They can withstand heavy snowfall, ice, and strong winds, providing long-lasting protection for your property.
  • Timeless Beauty: Slate roofs offer timeless elegance and aesthetic appeal, perfectly complementing the architectural styles found throughout New England. The natural variations in color and texture of slate create a unique and visually striking roof that enhances the overall charm of your property.
  • Low Maintenance: Slate roofs require minimal maintenance, saving you time and money in the long run. They are highly resistant to mold, mildew, and pests, eliminating the need for frequent repairs or treatments. With proper installation and periodic inspections, your slate roof can retain its pristine condition for decades.
  • Enhanced Property Value: Installing a slate roof can significantly increase the value of your property. Its durability, beauty, and long lifespan make it an attractive feature for potential buyers. A well-maintained slate roof adds prestige and curb appeal, setting your property apart in the competitive New England real estate market.

HOW WE WORK

Top Working Process

It's important to note that slate roof installation is a complex process that requires the expertise of experienced professionals. These three points provide a simplified overview, but proper installation techniques may vary depending on specific factors such as roof design, slope, and regional requirements.

1

Preparation

The installation process begins with thorough preparation, including the assessment of the roof structure, removal of existing materials, and ensuring a stable and solid foundation for the slate roof.


2

Underlayment and Flashing

A high-quality underlayment is carefully applied to provide an additional layer of protection against moisture. Flashing materials are strategically installed around chimneys, vents, and other roof penetrations to prevent water infiltration and ensure a watertight seal.

3

Slate Installation

Each slate tile is meticulously installed, starting from the bottom and working upwards, following a predetermined pattern and overlapping technique. Expert craftsmen precisely cut and fit each slate tile to create a visually appealing and structurally sound roof. Copper nails or hooks are used to secure the slates in place, ensuring long-term stability.
